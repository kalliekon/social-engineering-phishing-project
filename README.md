# Social Engineering & Phishing Simulation Project

Social engineering and phishing simulation project focused on human risk and cybersecurity awareness.

## Overview
This project explores how phishing attacks exploit human behavior and how organizations can reduce risk through awareness and structured processes.

## Phishing Scenario
**Subject:** Urgent: Password Reset Required  

Dear employee,

We have detected unusual activity on your account. Please reset your password immediately using the link below to avoid suspension.

[Reset Password]

IT Support Team

---

## Analysis
- The email creates urgency (“urgent”, “immediately”)  
- It impersonates a trusted entity (IT Support)  
- It pressures the user to act quickly without verification  
- It targets sensitive information (login credentials)  

---

## Risk Perspective
- Compromised credentials can lead to unauthorized access  
- Weak identity verification increases risk of account takeover  
- Human error is a key factor in security breaches  

---

## Mitigation Strategies
- User awareness training on phishing detection  
- Multi-factor authentication (MFA)  
- Clear identity verification processes  
- Reporting mechanisms for suspicious emails  

---

## Conclusion
This project highlights the importance of human awareness in cybersecurity and how structured processes can reduce security risks related to social engineering.
